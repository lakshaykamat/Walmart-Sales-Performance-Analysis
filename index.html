Project Background

Walmart, established in 1962, is a global retail leader specializing in groceries and general merchandise. Operating through physical stores, a robust e-commerce platform, and mobile applications, Walmart has built an extensive presence worldwide.

This project leverages Walmart’s extensive sales and operational data to generate actionable insights. These findings aim to enhance business efficiency, optimize inventory, and improve profitability.

Data Overview

The dataset consists of four key tables: Orders, Products, Sales, and Customers, with a total of 9,995 records.

Quality checks were conducted to ensure data accuracy, including checks for missing values, data types, and outliers.

Executive Summary

Key Takeaways:

  • Products priced between ₹0-₹500 dominate sales and profits.
  • The West region leads in total sales, followed by the East.
  • Sales peak in December, November, and September.

Sales Trends

Products in the ₹0-₹500 range generate 35% of total sales and 34% of profits. Sales peak in December, November, and September, with the West region leading (33%) followed by the East (28%).

Product-Level Performance

The Canon Printer is the most profitable product, generating ₹61.6K in sales and ₹25.2K in profits. However, CISCO EX90 shows a concerning loss of ₹1.8K despite ₹22.6K in sales.

Regional Comparisons

The West region leads in sales with 33%, driven by the Consumer segment (53%), followed by the Corporate segment (33%). Similarly, the East region contributes 28%, with the Consumer segment dominating at 55%.

Recommendations

  • Focus on products priced ₹0-₹500, especially during peak sales months.
  • Target marketing campaigns in the West and East regions, emphasizing the Consumer segment.
  • Optimize shipping processes to reduce delays, especially on high-volume days like Wednesday and Friday.
  • Reassess pricing strategies for underperforming products like CISCO EX90.
